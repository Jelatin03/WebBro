Logo History

1947 - 1951

Seventy-two years ago, a promising new basketball team emerged in Pennsylvania known as the Philadelphia Warriors. In 1947, just a year after their debut, the team unveiled its initial emblemâ€”a cartoon depiction of a Native American engaged in basketball, adorned with a feathered headdress. The color scheme was straightforward: yellow for the ball and feather, white for the Native American figure, and purple outlines. The logo also featured a handwritten-style yellow "Warriors" inscription.

1952 - 1962

During the final decade of their tenure in Philadelphia, the Warriors basketball team adopted a modified version of their original logo. Following the redesign, all full-color elements were eliminated, and the image now solely comprises blue outlines. Artists reworked the logo, altering proportions, with the ball's motion now resembling the letter "W" from the word "Warriors." The team name, Philadelphia Warriors, was positioned at the center of the emblem.

1963 - 1969

The updated logo emerged as the team relocated to a different state and adopted the name San Francisco Warriors. Designers crafted the emblem by incorporating a substantial circle as the foundation, within which a smaller circle was positioned. At the center, the national Indian headdress was prominently featured. Encircling the central yellow portion was a white ring, bearing the club's full name in blue sans-serif letters.

1970 - 1971

In 1969, the creators of the emblem chose to shift focus from the Native American theme and highlight the team's geographical connection. To achieve this, they illustrated the Golden Gate Bridge within a yellow circle bordered by blue, with the inscription "The CITY" positioned at the top.

1972 - 1975

In 1971, the team underwent a name change to Golden State Warriors, and this transformation was mirrored in the logo. The circular emblem now encompasses the outlines of the U.S. state of California, featuring a blue star marking the location of the San Francisco Bay Area to signify the team's geographical affiliation. Positioned above the orange circle is the dark blue text "GOLDEN STATE," while below it reads the word "WARRIORS."

1976 - 1988

For a span of 12 years, basketball players from California proudly represented their team under this emblem. Subsequently, artists redesigned the logo by creating a circle with lines to resemble a basketball. Additionally, they adjusted the color palette, substituting yellow for the previous orange.

1989 - 1997

The 1989 Golden State Warriors emblem evolved into a more polished rendition of its predecessor. The circle reverted to an orange hue, and the font underwent a transformation, with the letters adopting a flatter and bolder style compared to the previous versions.

1998 - 2010

In 1998, the Golden State Warriors completely overhauled their emblem, departing from the traditional design. The orange circle transformed into a lifelike basketball, serving as the backdrop for a figure clad in a blue superhero costume. This character, positioned over the word "WARRIORS," is depicted holding a zipper, and the lightning motif seamlessly integrates with the letter "W," appearing as its extension.

2011 - 2019

On July 17, 2010, a new logo was unveiled after 18 months of development. The team reverted to a yellow-blue color scheme, utilizing Warriors Royal Blue and California Golden Yellow as the primary hues. Designers incorporated a segment of the Golden Gate Bridge within a circle, featuring the inscription "GOLDEN STATE" at the top and "WARRIORS" at the bottom. This emblem emerged from a collaborative effort between the club, the NBA, and Adidas.

2020 - Present

In 2020, a shift to darker colors provided an opportunity to highlight the intricate details of the bridge. The revised logo was unveiled on June 14, 2019, coinciding with the team's relocation to the Chase Center.
