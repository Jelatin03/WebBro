Championships




2015 NBA Champions



After a four-decade-long break, the Golden State Warriors reclaimed the championship title. The team, which boasted the NBA's best regular season record, sealed their victory in Game 6 of the NBA Finals against the Cleveland Cavaliers. They secured a 105-97 win at Quicken Loans Arena, marking their fourth franchise championship and their first since 1975.


Andre Iguodala, who had not started a game in the playoffs until the Finals, earned the series MVP accolade for his exceptional performance. He averaged 16.3 points and played outstanding defense against LeBron James. He also averaged 5.8 rpg, 4.0 apg. Stephen Curry contributed 25 points and joined the exclusive group of players who won their first MVP and first title in the same season, a feat last accomplished by Shaquille O'Neal in 1999-2000.


Steve Kerr, the Warriors' head coach, made history as the first rookie head coach to win an NBA title since Pat Riley achieved this feat in 1981-82 with the Los Angeles Lakers. The Warriors culminated one of the most remarkable seasons in NBA history, finishing with an 83-20 record across the regular season and postseason, ranking as the third-best record behind the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls (87-13) and the 1996-97 Bulls (84-17).


Following this memorable moment in the Finals, the Golden State Warriors endured a lengthy 40-year period filled with disappointments, letdowns, and heartbreak. However, in the 2014-15 season, they reclaimed their top position. On June 16, 2015, the Warriors defeated the Cavaliers in Game 6 of The Finals, securing their first NBA championship since 1975.


With Stephen Curry, their first MVP since Wilt Chamberlain, leading the way, the Warriors dominated the highly competitive Western Conference. They entered the postseason as the No. 1 seed, swept New Orleans, overcame a 2-1 deficit against Memphis, and then defeated Houston in five games to reach the finals for the first time since 1975. In the title-clinching victory, Curry and Andre Iguodala each contributed 25 points, with Iguodala earning NBA Finals MVP honors for his exceptional performance, including his primary role in defending Cavs star LeBron James.


“This is special,” Curry said after Game 6. “To be able to hold this trophy and all the hard work we’ve put into it this season, this is special. We’re definitely a great team and a team that should go down in history as one of the best teams from top to bottom.”
