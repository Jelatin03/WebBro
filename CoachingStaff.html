Steve Kerr | Head Coach

Steve Kerr, born on September 27, 1965, in Beirut, Lebanon, is the Head Coach of the Golden State Warriors, taking up the position on May 19, 2014. In his initial years, he led the team to remarkable success, including five consecutive NBA Finals appearances and two back-to-back NBA Championships in 2017 and 2018. Kerr's coaching skills were acknowledged when he received the NBA Coach of the Year Award in 2016, making him the third coach in Warriors history to win this prestigious title. Prior to coaching, Kerr had a successful playing career, notably with the Chicago Bulls and the San Antonio Spurs, winning multiple NBA titles.

Kerr's basketball journey began at Palisades Charter High School in Los Angeles, California, and continued at the University of Arizona from 1983 to 1988. He helped the Wildcats reach the Final Four in the NCAA tournament and set an impressive NCAA 3-point percentage record of 57%. Kerr was later drafted by the Phoenix Suns in the 1988 NBA Draft and played for various teams during his 15-year NBA career, including the Suns, Cavaliers, Magic, Bulls, Spurs, and Trail Blazers. Off the court, Kerr is a family man, married to Margot, and they have three children named Nick, Maddy, and Matthew.

Kris Weems | Assistant Coach

Kris Weems, originally from California, currently serves as the assistant player development coach for the Golden State Warriors (GSW). Weems is an alumnus of Stanford University and has been associated with the Warriors since 2011 when he began learning the intricacies of player development during his college years. After initially joining the Warriors as a player development staff member, Weems transitioned to the role of a scout for the GSW. However, he ultimately left this position in 2014, subsequently taking up the responsibilities of both assistant coach and head coach for the Santa Cruz Warriors, the GSW's G League affiliate team.

Weems's journey in the basketball world gained momentum when he secured a position as a player development coach in the NBA in 2021. Prior to this achievement, he spent six years working within the minor league system of the Golden State Warriors. His perseverance and dedication paid off when he contributed to the team's NBA championship victory in 2022. Although Weems's own career as a player might not have been illustrious, he leveraged his passion for the sport and accumulated an extensive understanding of basketball through training and mentoring experiences, ranging from school and college to professional athletes. His residence in California has further facilitated his role in the development and enhancement of players within the Golden State Warriors organization.

Jacob Rubin | Assistant Coach

Jacob Rubin, who recently celebrated his 31st birthday, currently holds the position of Co-Head Video Coordinator & Player Development at the Golden State Warriors. Previously, from October 2015 to September 2017, Jacob served as the Manager of Basketball Operations for the Santa Cruz Warriors. Before this role, he gained experience as an intern at Wasserman Media Group in two separate stints, one from May 2014 to August 2014 and the other from May 2013 to August 2013. Additionally, Jacob interned for the NBA Summer League in July 2015 and for the University of Arizona Men's Basketball team from August 2010 to May 2011.

Jacob Rubin earned a Bachelor's Degree in Business and Sports Management from the University of Arizona - Eller College of Management.

Within the Golden State Warriors organization, Jacob Rubin collaborates with Ralph Walker, the Director of Team Security, Pabail Sidhu, the Director of Basketball Analytics, and Kent Lacob, the Director of Team Development. Jacob Rubin is under the supervision of Kirk Lacob, the Executive Vice President of Basketball Operations.

Kenny Atkinson | Assistant Coach

Born on June 2, 1967, Kenny Atkinson, 56 years old, is an American professional basketball coach and former player, currently serving as an assistant coach for the Golden State Warriors in the National Basketball Association (NBA) since 2021. Atkinson's coaching career saw a notable milestone with the Golden State Warriors when he helped the team secure a championship victory shortly after joining in 2021. Before transitioning into coaching, Kenny Atkinson had an extensive playing career with various teams outside the NBA, amassing over a decade of collective playing experience.

He made his coaching debut as an assistant coach for the New York team, where he spent four years before going on to work with other notable teams such as the Hawks, Nets, and Clippers, eventually finding his place at the Chase Center with the Warriors. Atkinson has actively been involved in leading the team during the Summer League matches, demonstrating his capabilities both behind the scenes and in the absence of the head coach, Steve Kerr. Remaining committed to the Warriors for the past two years, Atkinson reportedly declined an offer from the Charlotte Hornets in 2022, underscoring his dedication to the Golden State team.

Chris DeMarco | Assistant Coach

Chris DeMarco, 37 years old, born on September 26, 1986, was recruited by Kerr to become a part of the coaching team for the Golden State Warriors. DeMarco, who completed his MBA and was previously a college player, hails from Wisconsin and graduated from Dominican University in California. His initial role involved scouting teams and analyzing their game footage to devise effective strategies. Initially serving as a video coordinator, DeMarco's valuable insights and contributions were pivotal in helping the Warriors exploit their opponents' defensive weaknesses, ultimately enabling the team to secure a substantial lead in the playoffs.

DeMarco became a member of the Golden State Warriors in 2012 and later advanced to his current role in 2015. Aside from his athletic achievements, he also excelled academically, earning an MBA degree from his university. His profound understanding of basketball dynamics, coupled with his strategic business acumen, has undoubtedly proven to be an invaluable asset to the Warriors' management.

Drew Yoder | Trainer

Drew Yoder, a 34-year-old professional, assumes the role of a trainer in the NBA, yet his responsibilities extend far beyond the conventional scope of this title. Yoder holds a crucial position within the medical staff, primarily focusing on managing players' injuries, enhancing their performance, and overseeing their athletic training programs. He completed his education at Southern Methodist University and subsequently commenced his career as an athletic trainer with the Golden State Warriors.

In recognition of his significant contributions and dedicated service to the team over the course of ten years, Yoder was promoted to the esteemed position of Director of Medical Services by the Warriors. His tenure with the team has enabled him to play a pivotal role in addressing critical moments such as Curry's ankle injuries, Thompson's challenges, and Draymond's various medical concerns. Yoder's expertise and comprehensive understanding of sports medicine have undoubtedly been instrumental in maintaining the well-being and optimal performance of the players within the organization.

Bruce Fraser | Assistant Coach

Bruce Fraser, 59 years old, born on December 18, 1964, is a member of the coaching staff for the GSW, renowned as one of the most senior assistant coaches specifically recruited by Kerr. Fraser's noteworthy reputation stems from his significant contributions to the development of players, particularly in the case of Curry. It is widely acknowledged that Steph's exemplary discipline and exceptional shooting abilities were honed under the guidance of Kerr and Fraser, with particular emphasis on refining shooting mechanics.

His pivotal role involved overseeing and guiding the NBA MVP in his rigorous 3-point shooting drills, which included the practice of taking 500 shots. Fraser's instrumental involvement in fostering player development and maintaining effective communication within the team during Steve's absence underscored his indispensable value.

Residing in San Diego, the coach's ability to connect with the team on an emotional level and empathize with the players' sentiments greatly contributed to the success of the GSW. Despite not having a professional basketball background, he shared in the team's NBA triumph on four occasions alongside the head coach.
