All-time Greats



Warriors Hall of Fame

Paul Arizin Wilt Chamberlain Chris Mullin

Who ranks among the greatest Golden State Warriors in history? Being one of the original NBA franchises, the Warriors boast a history as illustrious as any team in the league. Throughout their existence, some of the most outstanding players in NBA history have donned the Warriors' jersey, dating back to their days in Philadelphia. The team spent 16 seasons in Philadelphia before relocating to San Francisco in 1962. Among the notable figures of the early NBA era, the Warriors boasted Wilt Chamberlain, often referred to as "The Big Dipper," and widely regarded as one of the finest centers in basketball history. During his tenure as a Warrior, Chamberlain famously set an NBA record by scoring 100 points in a single game, an achievement celebrated as one of the greatest feats in all of sports.


In 1975, the Warriors clinched their second championship, thanks to the remarkable talent of Rick Barry, widely acknowledged as one of the all-time greats. Barry, often regarded as one of the finest small forwards in the history of basketball, was a prolific scorer who managed to excel despite grappling with knee injuries. His stature in the game was solidified when he was honored as one of the NBA's 50 greatest players in 1996. Notable NBA stars such as Chris Mullin, Tim Hardaway, and Baron Davis all had stints in Oakland during their careers.


Over the course of their history, the Golden State team has consistently ranked among the top offensive squads in the league. They established a dynasty in the 2010s, with a key trio comprising Stephen Curry, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green, securing three championships (in 2015, 2017, and 2018) within a five-year span.
