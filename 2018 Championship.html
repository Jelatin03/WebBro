Championships




2018 NBA Champions



The Golden State Warriors clinched their third championship in four years by completing a four-game sweep of the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2018 NBA Finals. Kevin Durant claimed his back-to-back Finals MVP Award averaging 28.8 ppg, 10.8 rpg, and 7.5 apg. This latest victory has prompted discussions about the potential emergence of a new dynasty. While other NBA teams scramble during the summer to assemble the right pieces to challenge the league's giants, let's explore how the Warriors managed to cruise through the regular season and elevate their performance in the playoffs.


The Warriors secured the second spot in the Western Conference with a 58-24 record this season without exerting their full effort. Following their 2015-16 season, where they narrowly missed the championship, the team recognized that their talented roster could easily secure a playoff berth every year. Their primary objective was to hit their peak performance, often referred to as "clicking like Golden State," when it mattered most: during the postseason.


Despite facing a challenging series against the Houston Rockets in the Western Conference Finals, the Warriors always seemed to have control of the situation. By the time they reached the Finals, it was evident that the Cavaliers had a slim chance of defeating them four times in a seven-game series. In what could potentially be their last Finals matchup, the Warriors dispatched Cleveland with ease. Kevin Durant earned Finals MVP honors for the second consecutive season, averaging over 30 points and nine rebounds per game while shooting an impressive 54 percent from the field.


Durant's unique skill set and physical presence have proven to be nearly unstoppable, even when his teammates faced offensive challenges. One notable statistic from KD's two-year Finals performance is that, prior to Game 4 in this year's series, he hadn't scored fewer than 26 points in a single game. In the game where he fell slightly short of that mark, he contributed 20 points, secured 12 rebounds, and provided ten assists. His remarkable consistency has arguably been the most significant factor in the team's championship victories. While containing both Steph Curry and Klay Thompson on the same night is already a daunting task, adding Durant to the equation makes it nearly impossible for opponents to defend against the Warriors effectively.
