2017 NBA Champions



The Golden State Warriors secured the 2017 NBA Championship with a 129-120 victory over the Cleveland Cavaliers in Game 5 of the NBA Finals on Monday night. During the second quarter, the Warriors orchestrated an impressive 15-0 scoring run, turning a deficit into a commanding double-digit lead, and they maintained their lead for the rest of the game. Kevin Durant's performance was exceptional, amassing 39 points, 7 rebounds, and 5 assists, earning him the title of the 2017 NBA Finals MVP. Stephen Curry contributed 34 points, 6 rebounds, and 10 assists, while Andre Iguodala delivered an outstanding performance off the bench, scoring 20 points in the 2017 postseason's highest-scoring game.


Durant's quest for his first championship was nothing short of spectacular, as he scored 39 points, secured seven rebounds, and provided five assists while also defending against LeBron James, who contributed 41 points, 13 rebounds, and eight assists. He claimed the Finals MVP award averaging 35.2 ppg, 8.2 rpg, and 5.4 apg. The back-to-back MVP, Steph Curry, also ended the Finals on a high note, rebounding from a challenging Game 4 to score 34 points, distribute 10 assists, and grab six rebounds.


One of the most memorable moments in the Finals came during the early fourth quarter when Durant took the opportunity to secure his first championship. LeBron James had reduced Golden State's lead to just three points with a remarkable drive and reverse layup past Durant. However, Durant quickly responded, sinking a difficult one-legged stepback jumper over James. He then capitalized on the attention he drew from the Cavaliers' defense by delivering a crosscourt pass to Andre Iguodala, who scored a three-pointer, extending the lead to eight points. Durant continued to shine, sinking another three-pointer on the next possession, as the Warriors executed precise ball movement. This shot narrowly eluded Kevin Love's outstretched hand.


As the game progressed, and with Curry back on the court after a bench stint, Durant continued to make crucial contributions. He slipped behind the defense, receiving a pass from Iguodala for an uncontested dunk that pushed the Warriors ahead 110-102. Later, he made a clean midrange shot that increased the lead to 116-106 and darted down the lane for a layup past Kyle Korver, keeping the Cavaliers alive.


Curry also played his part by being assertive when three-point opportunities were limited. With 4:13 remaining, and as the Cavaliers mounted a comeback, Durant spotted Curry cutting into the paint and made a precise pass from near the halfcourt line, leading to Curry's layup that extended the Warriors' lead to 14 points. Despite the exquisite basketball displayed by both teams in the past two Finals, there were often qualifications attached to the outcomes.
