Rodney Mcgruder

Height: 6'4ft | Weight: 205 lbs

Rodney McGruder, 32 years old, born on July 29, 1991, from Landover, Maryland, and currently plays as a shooting guard in the NBA. McGruder, who has two brothers named Ricardo and Renaldo, as well as a sister named Rashaun, attended Archbishop Carroll High School in Washington, D.C., and later pursued his education at Arlington Country Day in Jacksonville, Florida. He played college basketball at Kansas State for all four years, gaining recognition as a preseason All-Big 12 pick during his senior year in 2012-13.

Following his college career, McGruder went undrafted in the 2013 NBA draft but joined the Orlando Magic for the Orlando Summer League and the Charlotte Bobcats for the Las Vegas Summer League. Subsequently, he signed with the Oklahoma City Thunder in September 2013 but was later waived by the team in October of the same year. He then played for Atomerőmű SE in Hungary for the 2013-14 season before moving on to the G-League, where he played for the Warriors' and Heat's teams, the latter of which won the G-League Championship in the 2015-2016 season.

McGruder finally made his NBA debut with the Miami Heat on October 26, 2016. However, his progress was hindered by a stress fracture in his left leg, causing him to be ruled out indefinitely on October 12, 2017. He made his return for the Heat during the 2018-2019 season but was eventually waived by the team on April 7, 2019. Soon after, on April 9, 2019, he was claimed off waivers by the Los Angeles Clippers, where he signed a 3-year, $15 million contract on July 10, 2019, after the team extended a $3,021,354 qualifying offer.
