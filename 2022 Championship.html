2022 NBA Champions



The Golden State Warriors secured the 2022 NBA Championship by defeating the Boston Celtics with a score of 103-90 in Game 6 of the NBA Finals, held at TD Garden Arena in Boston, Massachusetts. With this victory, the Warriors have now joined the ranks of the Boston Celtics and the Los Angeles Lakers as one of the few NBA franchises to have won seven or more championship titles. Their journey in the 2022 NBA Playoffs saw them achieve a 16-6 record (.727), including a 4-2 victory over the Boston Celtics in the NBA Finals, a 4-1 win over the Dallas Mavericks in the Western Conference Finals, a 4-2 triumph over the Memphis Grizzlies in the Semifinals, and a 4-1 victory over the Denver Nuggets in the First Round.


Despite initially falling behind 14-2 in the first quarter, the Warriors made a remarkable comeback with a 21-0 scoring run, marking the longest such run in a Finals game in the past 50 years, as reported by the Elias Sports Bureau. Heading into the second half, the Warriors held a 15-point lead and maintained their advantage throughout the game. Leading the way for Golden State was Stephen Curry, who contributed 34 points, 7 rebounds, and 7 assists.


Warriors guard Stephen Curry was honored with the Bill Russell NBA Finals Most Valuable Player Award for his exceptional performance, averaging 31.2 points, 6.0 rebounds, 5.0 assists, and 2.00 steals in 37.5 minutes per game during The Finals. Curry now joins an exclusive group of NBA legends as the seventh player in NBA history to have achieved at least four NBA titles and two Most Valuable Player awards. This illustrious list includes Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Michael Jordan, Bill Russell, LeBron James, Magic Johnson, and Tim Duncan. Notably, Curry, Klay Thompson, Draymond Green, and Andre Iguodala each earned their fourth NBA Championship, making them part of the select group of 40 all-time NBA players (0.8 percent) who have won four NBA titles. They are the second quartet, in the last 50 years, to collectively secure four championships, joining Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Michael Cooper, Magic Johnson, and Kurt Rambis, who accomplished this feat in 1982, 1985, 1987, and 1988.
