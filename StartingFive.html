Stephen Curry

Height: 6'2ft | Weight: 185 lbs

Stephen Curry, also known as Steph Curry, 35 years old, is an accomplished American professional basketball player who was born on March 14, 1988, in Akron, Ohio. He is renowned for his role in leading the Golden State Warriors to NBA championships in 2014–15, 2016–17, 2017–18, and 2021–22, including a historic 73–9 regular-season record in 2015–16.

Despite facing initial doubts about his physical stature, Curry's exceptional shooting skills and deep understanding of the game were evident from an early age, influenced by his father, former NBA player Dell Curry. Although he did not receive offers from major college basketball programs, he attended Davidson College and quickly made an impact, gaining national attention for leading the team to the Elite Eight of the NCAA tournament during his sophomore year.

In the NBA, Curry continued to excel, earning multiple MVP awards and leading the league in three-pointers made. His impressive performances on the court, including record-breaking three-point shots and high-scoring averages, solidified his reputation as one of the most exceptional players in the league.

Throughout his career, Curry has experienced both triumphs and setbacks, including injuries that temporarily sidelined him. Despite these challenges, he continued to contribute significantly to his team's success, showcasing his skills and leadership abilities, which ultimately led to further championship victories for the Golden State Warriors.

Klay Thompson

Height: 6'6ft | Weight: 220 lbs

Klay Alexander Thompson, 33 years old, born on February 8, 1990, is a well-known professional basketball player, born to a family deeply immersed in sports. His father, Mychal Thompson, was a former NBA player, and his mother, Julie, played volleyball at the University of San Francisco. His older brother, Mychel, also had a brief stint in the NBA, while his younger brother, Trayce, played Major League Baseball.

Thompson himself achieved notable success in basketball, particularly with the Golden State Warriors in the NBA. He was selected as the 11th overall pick in the 2011 NBA Draft. Over the course of his career, Thompson has been a crucial part of the Warriors' success, contributing to their four NBA championships. He has also been recognized for his sharpshooting skills, winning the Three-Point Contest at the 2016 NBA All-Star Saturday Night and leading the NBA in three-pointers made during the 2018-2019 season.

Thompson has also been a part of the United States national basketball team, winning an Olympic gold medal in 2016 and a gold medal at the 2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup in Spain. He has been selected as an All-Star multiple times and has set various NBA records, including the single-game record for most 3-point field goals made and attempted.

Thompson's contributions to the sport have solidified his place as one of the top players in the league, known not only for his scoring abilities but also for his consistent three-point shooting prowess.

Chris Paul

Height: 6'0ft | Weight: 175 lbs

Christopher Emmanuel Paul, also known as CP3, 38 years old, is an accomplished American professional basketball player born on May 6, 1985, in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. Paul is widely recognized for his exceptional skills as a point guard, challenging the conventional notion of the "pure point guard" with his ability to score as effectively as he can set up his teammates.

During his high school and college years, Paul showcased his remarkable talent and competitive spirit, earning recognition as one of the top prospects in the country. He was selected fourth overall by the New Orleans Hornets in the 2005 NBA Draft, where he immediately made an impact and won the Rookie of the Year award. Despite facing challenges such as injuries and a trade controversy with the Los Angeles Lakers, Paul's leadership and skill on the court remained evident as he continued to excel with the New Orleans Hornets and later with the Los Angeles Clippers.

Paul's career also included a successful stint with the Houston Rockets before being traded to the Oklahoma City Thunder and then the Phoenix Suns, where he played a crucial role in the team's success. With the Suns, he led the team to the NBA Finals in the 2020-2021 season, marking his first appearance in the championship series. However, the Suns were ultimately defeated by the Milwaukee Bucks.

Aside from his achievements in the NBA, Paul also represented the United States in the 2008 and 2012 Olympic Games, contributing to the teams' gold medal victories. Despite various setbacks, Paul's legacy in basketball remains significant, with his exceptional skills and leadership continuing to inspire fans and players alike.

Draymond Green

Height: 6'6ft | Weight: 230 lbs

Draymond Green, 33 years old, born on March 4, 1990, known by the nickname "Day-Day," is a successful professional basketball player born to Mary Babers, Raymond Green, and Wallace Davis. He has two brothers, Torrian Harris and Braylon Green, as well as three sisters, LaToya Babers, Jordan Davis, and Gabby Davis. Green has been recognized for his contributions on and off the court, including his induction into the Michigan State Athletics Hall of Fame in 2022 and the retirement of his jersey numbers at both Saginaw High School and Michigan State University.

He is also known for hosting basketball camps for youth, including the Draymond Green Basketball Camp and All-Star Game in Grand Rapids, Michigan, as well as his involvement in various media projects, such as his podcast "The Draymond Green Show" and his multi-year agreement with Turner Network Television (TNT) Sports to contribute to "Inside the NBA" and provide exclusive content to Turner Sports and Bleacher Report. Outside of his professional endeavors, Green remains connected to his hometown of Saginaw, Michigan, where he once worked as a janitor at Ruben Daniels Middle School.

Green has had a successful career with the Golden State Warriors, where he has been an integral part of the team's multiple NBA championships. He has earned several accolades, including being named the NBA Defensive Player of the Year, making the All-NBA Team, and being selected as an All-Star. He holds various franchise records for the Warriors, including most career regular-season triple-doubles and ranking among the top leaders in steals, assists, blocks, rebounds, defensive rebounds, and 3-pointers made.

Andrew Wiggins

30 | Guard

Height: 6'7ft | Weight: 197 lbs

Andrew Christian Wiggins, 28 years old, born on February 23, 1995, a Canadian professional basketball player, was born in Toronto. His family has a strong background in sports, with his father, Mitchell, having played in the NBA and his mother, Marita, being an Olympic track star. Wiggins began his high school career in Ontario before transferring to Huntington Prep in West Virginia, where he garnered significant attention for his outstanding performance.

He then played for the University of Kansas before entering the 2014 NBA Draft, where he was selected as the first overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers but was subsequently traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves. Wiggins has been involved in various philanthropic endeavors, including donating to charity based on his scoring performance and running the Andrew Wiggins Foundation, which supports youth athletic organizations.

During his time at Kansas, Wiggins received several accolades, including being named to the All-Big 12 first team, the All-Big 12 Tournament first team, and winning the Big 12 Rookie of the Year award. He set a Kansas freshman record for points scored and played a pivotal role in leading the Jayhawks to the 2013-14 regular season Big 12 title and the second round of the 2014 NCAA Tournament.
